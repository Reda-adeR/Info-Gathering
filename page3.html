Basic Concepts on Soil Fertility

Soil Thing Friendly Meaning Why Plants Care
Nitrogen (N) The “leafy green” food that helps plants grow tall and dark green like spinach for humans. Without enough N, leaves turn pale and growth slows.
Phosphorus (P) Gives plants energy to make strong roots and healthy seeds like batteries in a toy. Low P means weak roots and few fruits.
Potassium (K) Helps plants stay healthy and fight “colds” (disease) like vitamins for us. Good K makes fruits bigger and plants tougher in drought.
Soil pH How sour or sweet the soil juice is, on a scale of 0–14. Most crops like “just right” 6–7; too sour locks up nutrients.
Organic Matter Rotten leaves & compost that make soil fluffy and full of snacks. Feeds microbes, holds water, and slowly releases nutrients.
Soil Texture Whether soil feels sandy, silty (flour), or clayey (sticky). Affects water holding: sand drains fast, clay holds water but can drown roots.

Fun fact: farmers call Nitrogen, Phosphorus, and Potassium the “N‑P‑K trio.” Fertiliser bags show them as three numbers, like 10‑5‑15.

SoilGrids is a collections of soil property maps for the world produced using machine learning at 250 m resolution. Predictions are made at six standard depths. SoilGrids uses global models that are calibrated using all available input observations and globally available environmental covariates.

Soil‑Fertility Snapshot – Get N P K, pH, OM & Texture for Your Land

Follow these 4 quick steps – no lab visit required.

1

Draw or upload your field polygon

Use any geo tool (QGIS, Google Earth, Farm OS) to export a .geojson or capture a simple bbox [minLon, minLat, maxLon, maxLat].

2

Pick a soil API

  • iSDAsoil 30 m – best detail across Africa.
  • SoilGrids 250 m – global, free, six depth layers.
  • OpenLandMap – texture & organic C extras.
3

Query the endpoint

GET https://isdasoil.io/api/v1/soil?lon=36.82&lat=-1.29&property=ph,nitrogen,potassium or POST your polygon to get a full table (JSON or GeoTIFF). Expect keys like ph_h2o, soc, n_tot.

4

Visualise & decide actions

Map pH classes (e.g. acid < 5.5) → lime plan. Overlay N & organic C → variable‑rate fertiliser. Texture (% sand/silt/clay) drives irrigation zones.

🔧 Tip for devs: cache the soil API response locally—soil properties change slowly, so one call can serve months of app sessions.
